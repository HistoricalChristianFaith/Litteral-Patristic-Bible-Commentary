1:11

‎1:12‎  ‎1:13‎  ‎1:14‎  ‎1:15‎  ‎1:16‎  ‎1:17‎  ‎1:18‎  ‎1:19‎  ‎1:20‎  
 
 
 
Saying: What thou seest, write in a book, and send to the seven churches which are in Asia, to Ephesus, and to Smyrna, and to Pergamus, and to Thyatira, and to Sardis, and to Philadelphia, and to Laodicea.
 
 
St. Victorinus
 
In the whole world Paul taught that all the churches are arranged by sevens, that they are called seven, and that the Catholic Church is one. And first of all, indeed, that he himself also might maintain the type of seven churches, he did not exceed that number. But he wrote to the Romans, to the Corinthians, to the Galatians, to the Ephesians, to the Thessalonians, to the Philippians, to the Colossians; afterwards he wrote to individual persons, so as not to exceed the number of seven churches. And abridging in a short space his announcement, he thus says to Timothy: "That thou mayest know how thou oughtest to behave thyself in the Church of the living God.(John 3:33-34)" We read also that this typical number is announced by the Holy Spirit by the mouth of Isaiah: "Of seven women which took hold of one man.(Isa. 4:1)" The one man is Christ, not born of seed; but the seven women are seven churches, receiving His bread, and clothed with his apparel, who ask that their reproach should be taken away, only that His name should be called upon them. The bread is the Holy Spirit, which nourishes to eternal life, promised to them, that is, by faith. And His garments wherewith they desire to be clothed are the glory of immortality, of which Paul the apostle says: "For this corruptible must put on incorruption, and this mortal must put on mortality.(1Cor. 15:53)" Moreover, they ask that their reproach may be taken away — that is, that they may be cleansed from their sins: for the reproach is the original sin which is taken away in baptism, and they begin to be called Christian men, which is, "Let thy name be called upon us." Therefore in these seven churches, of one Catholic Church are believers, because it is one in seven by the quality of faith and election.

Primasius
 
"I believe the work may be undertaken to harmonize -- if we cannot win all, at least to study -- the certain names of the seven churches, that interpreted into our tongue, they may resound; so that by the their titles, the indications may reveal facts to us, supporting also the considered divine words, fitting one's sense also of the character of each church, or joined to what is remembered of the churches.

"And first what is done about Asia, in which are these seven churches counted, just as in the age. For "Asia" is interpreted "elation/lifting up". And so the whole Church in this world is warned, by being placed in the province in "elation", so that elation will be rejected by it; one may accept being placed under the "easy" yoke of meek Christ; or one may accomplish a more spacious cultivation of piety by those who have already begun.

"So proud Saul was disclosed when the Lord rebuked him, so that he acquired humility; and he handed over "Paul" (Paulus) as if he were a little bit ("paululum"); so that he who recently was a hungry wolf who panted for prey in Christ's disciples, he actually bent the neck to devoted Ananias (which is interpreted "sheep"), and the future teacher would be placed under the humble discipleship of preaching the faith.

"Therefore "Ephesus" is said to be interpreted "will" or "my counsel". Truly, we knew it brought in "will" set up for charity, "counsel" for negligence's correction. And in the end this is made clear when they have described also what is pleasing to the will what they have appeased out of charity, and they are bound warningly by more of "my counsel."

"Pergamus, is said to be interpreted "their horns will be divided", or "the valleys will descend into speech." Formerly, because of its angel, they pointed out two of the appearance of men to those holding onto the faith, but to the others holding the Nicolaites' doctrine, not unjustly He divided the horns (that the same name resounds) between the just and the sinners, except for, as we read [Ps. 74], the horns of the sinners are broken, and the horns of the just were exalted. And the valley will descend. And by all means, “the humility of confession” will have discerned by the name of the valley, designated by their ruin, those who were called to a doctrine woven by Satan. For one ought to repent neglect of one’s soil, He says to them of the grave lapse to be tested out and pricked with remorse.

“Sardis is interpreted “prince of beauty”. In it, of course the Church remembers the worth of the elect, saying, “….who have not soiled their garments. They shall walk with me in white.”

“Philadelphia” is interpreted “saving”, “sticking to the Lord”, on account on this such words were directed, in which also he would have known himself loved, and will be protected from the hour of temptation.

“Laodicea” is said to be interpreted “tribe lovable to the Lord” or “they were in vomit.” Where He shows what is devoted to the service of divine charity; how by the weakness of human elation, which they threw away to be rich themselves, it was charged more justly. And finally, the Lord says to the lovable, “I who love you, accuse and castigate you; but on the contrary, one is accused of lukewarmness; so that the ugliness of transgression repulsed, the beloved may persevere, to whom also He promises the converted fiery gold and a white robe. But there, the impenitent heart that is carried was in vomit, of which also He says, “I will start to vomit thee out of My mouth, so that the word and deed may harmonize with the name.”

St. Andrew of Caesarea
 
The revelation is given to the seven churches comprising the metropolia of Ephesus, which St. John the Theologian then governed, having his permanent dwelling in Ephesus. But of course, in the person of these seven churches the revelation is also given to the whole Church. The number seven, moreover, has a mystical meaning, signifying completeness. Therefore it may be placed here as a symbol of the Ecumenical Church, to which as a whole the Apocalypse is addressed.

Apringius of Beja
 
There is a mighty mystery in the names which we will examine and discuss to the extent that God allows. Ephesus means “my will” or “my plan.” He wills that we know that the whole reality of our faith and the dignity of the Catholic Church is not to be ascribed to human merit, but they are the will of God and the disposition of the divine purpose. Smyrna means “their song.” And what else is the song of the perfect if not the celestial doctrine and the preaching of the gospel and the advance of the Christian religion, or the melodious confession of the Catholic Church? Pergamum means “to him who divides their horns.” This refers either to the insolence of the powers of the air, or to the arrogance of the heretics. And he teaches that the pride of the powers is always to be separated and divided from the congregation of the Church, for the horns are either power or arrogance. He writes to Thyatira, that is “enlightened.” This signifies that, after the expulsion of heretical pride and after the defeat of temptations from the powers of the air, the holy Church is deserving of the light of righteousness. Sardis means the “beginning of beauty.” The Church is seized by the sun of righteousness and is illumined by the light of truth, so that she might have the beginning of beauty, the Lord Jesus Christ, and might always shine in perpetual light. Philadelphia means, “preserving devotion to the Lord.” After possessing the sun of righteousness, after the illumination of holiness, after the comeliness of holy beauty, the Church rightly is devoted to the Lord and preserves herself by an inviolable observation of devotion. Laodicea means “a tribe beloved of the Lord,” or as some would have it, “a birth is expected.” Both are meaningful, for she who has merited the beauty of faith and the sun of righteousness, and knows that through faith the Lord cleaves to her, might also be a tribe whom the Lord loves who is both loved by the Lord and preserved by the Lord. Furthermore, the Church might well await her birth, either the regeneration of baptism, or the glory of the resurrection, whenever she preserves herself by humility and patience.

St. Bede
 
seven churches. The Church of Christ was not at the time in these places alone, but all fulness is comprised in the number seven. Asia, which is interpreted elevation, denotes the proud exaltation of the world in which the Church is sojourning, and, as is the method of the divine mystery, the genus is contained in the species. For the Apostle Paul also writes to seven churches, but not to the same as St. John. And although these seven churches are a sevenfold figure of the whole Church, still the things which he blames, or praises, came to pass in them one by one.

St. Beatus of Liébana

‘“What thou seest, write in a book.” And may it be understood that while the divine eloquence is heard, while impervious things are penetrated, the eyes are not more greatly pushed apart than the ears are opened; so that they may see hidden things, they may observe what is realized, and what has been seen, they are admonished that it should be noted down in books. Of which book, the Lord has spoken through the Prophet, saying, “I will give my law in their bowels, and I will write it in their heart: and I will be their God, and they shall be my people. And they shall teach no more every man his neighbour, and every man his brother, saying,’Know the Lord’, for all shall know me from the least of them even to the greatest, saith the Lord. For I will forgive their iniquity, and I will remember their sin no more.” [Jerem. 31:33]

‘Here is the book in which the Apostle is admonished to write what he has seen, to impress it upon the hearts of hearers, or be taught to understand it from memory. Of which book, the most blessed Teacher of the Gentiles [Paul] says, “You are our epistle, written in your hearts, which is known and read by all men.” [2 Cor 3:2]

‘And John is admonished where to send what he will write. “Send it,” He says, “to the seven churches.” Since we have said already there is one Church, it is said to all the world in that generation, that is, from that time until the consummation of the age to be.

‘Now, since the names of these same churches are picked specifically, we may see what intelligence each holds in itself. “To Ephesus”, He says, “Smyrna, and Pergamum, and Thyatira, and Sardis, and Philadelphia, and Laodicea.” Can it be that the perfection of Christian religion was obtained only in these cities, and has “their sound gone forth into all the earth, and their words unto the ends of the world”? [Psalm 18:5/19:4]

[The answer to a "numquid" question like that is "No."]

‘But perhaps there is a mystery in the names that we are discussing, that we will unlock so far as God will grant it.

‘“Ephesus” truly is called “my Will” or “my counsel”. He wished the standing of all our faith and the dignity of the Catholic Church to be known, not ascribing it to human merit but to the will of God and to have displayed the divine dispensation.

‘But “Smyrna” is called “their song”, and what other song of the perfect ones is there, besides the celestial doctrine, and the preaching of the Gospel, and the progress of the Christian religion, or indeed the melodious confession of the Catholic Church?

‘“Pergamum” sounds like “their divided horn”, or “the relentlessness of the powers of the air”, or “swellings of the heretics”; it shows the puffings up of the powerful always separate and divide a congregation from the Church. For “horn” is either ‘power’ or ‘swelling’.

‘And “Thyatira”, which is “illuminated”, means the Holy Church after the expulsion of heretics, after the swellings of the powerful, after the powers of the air, after the trials, are trampled down; the light of justice has been won.

‘“Sardis” is “the beginning of beauty”, for of course the Church is touched by the Sun of Justice; lit up by the lamp of Truth, it may have the beginning of beauty, the Lord Jesus Christ, in Whose light what is plucked up will bloom endlessly forever.

‘“Philadelphia”, which is interpreted “saving”, is sticking to the Lord after being touched by the Sun of Justice, after the holy illumination, after the appearance of holy beauty; the Church keeps to Her imperishable Lord by Her devotion and observance, for the merit of holding on.

‘“Laodicea” is “thrice beloved of the Lord”, or as certain ones prefer it, “the birth is awaited”. But either one means, that as she served the Sun of Justice by beauty of faith, and acknowledged the Lord through her faith to stick to Him, it also may be beloved of the Lord. And the tribe loved by the Lord is guarded by the Lord and directed by the Lord. And she may await her birth (or regeneration in Baptism); or she may endure with humility and patience till the glory of the resurrection.

The names are divided into these steps of the Church. Nor are the nouns of the listed cities written down in vain. For who is mentioned by all the world? Why does it matter so much to write to the seven churches? Here is what to understand of them, with what is said: the Lord has willed a mystery in the heavenly sacrament. These seven chosen names contain the Church of the whole world, as also the mystic number; and all the churches may reveal Her dignity.’

Nicholas of Lyra
