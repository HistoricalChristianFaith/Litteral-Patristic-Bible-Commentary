Fear none of those things which thou shalt suffer. Behold, the devil will cast some of you into prison that you may be tried: and you shall have tribulation ten days. Be thou faithful until death: and I will give thee the crown of life.
 
 
 
 
Tertullian
 
Keep for God His own property untainted; He will crown it if He choose. Nay, then, He does even choose. He calls us to it. To him who conquers He says, "I will give a crown Of life." Be you, too, faithful unto death, and fight you, too, the good fight, whose crown the (2Tim 4:8) apostle feels so justly confident has been laid up for him. [The Chaplet Chapter 15]

St. Cyprian
 
He who under the eyes of God has offered himself to tortures and to death, has suffered whatever he was willing to suffer; for it was not he that was wanting to the tortures, but the tortures that were wanting to him. "Whosoever shall confess me before men, him will I also confess before my Father which is in heaven,(Matt. 10:32)" saith the Lord. They have confessed Him "He that endureth to the end, the same shall be saved,(Matt. 1:22)" saith the Lord. They have endured and have carried the uncorrupted and unstained merits of their virtues through, even unto the end. And, again, it is written, "Be thou faithful unto death, and I will give thee a crown of life." They have persevered in their faithfulness, and stedfastness, and invincibleness, even unto death. When to the willingness and the confession of the name in prison and in chains is added also the conclusion of dying, the glory of the martyr is consummated. [Epistle 36 Verse 1] 
 

And even more strongly the Holy Spirit, teaching and showing that the army of the devil is not to be feared, and that, if the foe should declare war against us, our hope consists rather in that war itself; and that by that conflict the righteous attain to the reward of the divine abode and eternal salvation...And in the Apocalypse, divine protection is promised to our sufferings. 'Fear nothing of these things,' it says, 'which thou shall suffer.' (Exhortation 10)

Primasius
 
Behold, the devil is about to throw some of you into prison, that you may be tested, and for 10 days you will have tribulation. These words are fitting for the Catholic Church, against whom the Devil is always waging war with unavoidable hostilities. And for this reason the course of the present life is signified by the 10 days, since in the Decalogue there exists in brief form a summary of the Law. For here are the required Divine commandments where there is need for continence. For this reason also the Lord said, “Agree with your accuser,” that is, the Law, “while you are with him in the way of this age.(Matt. 5:25)” In this way he indicates the entire time when, with the Law instructing you, there is conflict against the manifold allurements of desire, so that when the 5 physical senses are subdued through the grace of God, victory is achieved. And when duplicated on account of the twofold sexes, the 5 senses become 10. I also think the 10 days may be interpreted as the total course of this life in which there will never fail to be a threefold temptation for the faithful. For ‘the life of man upon the earth is a trial,(Job 7:1)’ and this is so as the 7 days pass by and continue to pass by until the whole time of the present life is unfolded. The Devil was permitted to bring this temptation even upon our Lord Jesus Christ as the Head of the Church. This spiritual battle of the Church of Christ is further indicated in the figure of a woman who is in struggle. For the Lord said to the Serpent, “I will put enmity between you and the woman. She will watch your head, and you will lie in wait for her heel.(Gen. 3:15)” It is, therefore, clearly recognized that in this age, which is completed in course of 7 days, the Church of Christ is subjected to various trial. There receive their most powerful and principal expression in the 3 sins, in the passion of fleshly desire and the unrestrained gluttony of forbidden excesses. The enemy attempted to deceive the Lord by way of bread, as though He were a second Adam, since he persuaded him to taste what was forbidden to humankind. And so by rendering the 1st Adam a captive to the belly, he intended to bind him in addiction also to other desires of the flesh. And the enemy was also allowed to attack the Lord with the temptation to vainglory when he exhorted Him to throw Himself headlong from the pinnacle of the temple, adding the witness of the psalm which says, “He has given his angels charge over you” and the following (Ps. 91:11). And just as the enemy had deceived the first man with the suggestion, “You shall be like gods knowing good and evil,( Gen. 3:5)” so he exhorted the Lord to throw Himself down incautiously out of the desire to display the Deity which He had laid aside. And the enemy tempts also a 3rd time through which it is indicated that greed for the glory of earthy kingdoms and the pomp of worldly wealth must be condemned.(Lk. 4:1-13) When this 3-fold temptation is added to the times of this life, which are indicated by the 7 days, the number 10 is fulfilled, and for this reason it is said, “for 10 days you will have tribulation.” Be faithful unto death These words pertain to the entire Church. For also the Apostle teaches this when he speaks of Adam and says, “Adam was not deceived, but the woman was deceived and became a transgressor.(1Tim. 2:14)” And thereupon he indicated the Church by moving to figurative speech, saying, “She will be saved through the bearing of sons, if she continues in faith and love and holiness with modesty.(1Tim. 2:15)” No one believes that this is spoken of a woman who is dead, and not rather of the Church of Christ.

Apringius of Beja
 
But he says, “You will have tribulation for 10 days.” This is to say, should you consider the present evils which you suffer in comparison to the perpetuity of the future blessedness, you will regard these without doubt as small and as quickly transient as though of 10 duration. And therefore, the Apostle said; “I consider that the sufferings of this present time are not worth comparing with the glory that is to revealed to us.(Rom. 8:18)”

St. Bede

devil. These words apply to the Universal Church, against which the devil is always exercising an enmity from which there is no escape.

ten days. He means the whole of the time during which the commandments of the decalogue are necessary. For as long as thou shalt follow the light of the divine word, thou must needs suffer imprisonment from the enemy who opposes thee. Some understand that the ten persecutions of the heathen, from the Emperor Nero to Domitian, are signified.

death. He shews how far the ten days reach, in that he counsels them to keep the faith unto death.

St. Beatus of Liébana

Therefore, He exhorts the Church not to fear those who kill the body; and afterward, they have nothing that they would have done, saying; “Fear none of those things which thou shalt suffer.”

He signifies future troubles also will be thrown upon them wickedly by the impious. And He comforts His faithful; they are not to be frightened of the troubles of persecutions. But He says it as if to one person, since all the saints are one while they live in this world, because they are joined in Christ’s love in one soul and one heart, and there is one Church.

Since thus Christ and the Church, the head and the members, are one; so evil men with the devil who is their head, are one body. And when He says, “Fear none of those things which thou shalt suffer”, certainly He signifies both what he may be strong to suffer and what the Enemy can throw in from the whole body of the devil, that besieges the Church from within and without, through all the world.

“Behold, the devil will cast some of you into prison that you may be tried; and you shall have tribulation ten days.”

I think indeed that these things are not to be rashly said, or believed the way that not a few people have seen it or do see it — that no large number of the Church will suffer persecutions at the time of Antichrist, because of how she has suffered already — that is, the Church has been at Antichrist’s feet ten, eleven times in the same way.

Indeed, they reckon the first [persecution] that done by Nero, the second by Domitian, the third by Trajan, the fourth by Antoninus, the fifth by Severus, the sixth by Maximinus, the seventh by Decius, the eighth by Octavian, the ninth by Valerian, and the tenth by Diocletian and Maximian. By these ten kings, the Church suffered from the Ascension of Christ until the Council of Nicaea, for 250 years. Massacres were made of the martyrs, as through this Apocalypse, the Lord said to John: “you shall have tribulation ten days”, for the ten kings.

The devil stood changed into human appearance; against the Christians he said, “Why do you worship Jesus the crucified, a man of Judaea of no importance to men?” He incited the princes of the world so that they would bring death to whoever might believe in Christ. But after that, the Gospel was preached to the whole globe when those same kings whose laws had laid waste to the Church, had supplied martyrs to everyone (beneficially to them) who tried cruelly to remove them from the earth; and they began to attack the false gods for whose cause the worshippers of God truly were persecuted in the past; and to destroy the temples of the gods and to build basilicas in the name of the martyrs.

Seeing this, the devil put on vestments of religion under the name of Christianity, and from Christ fought against Christ. He stirred up heretics in the Church; and now there is an eleventh persecution by the Antichrist. After Christ’s Gospel had traveled over every part of the whole globe, surely the persecution in the Church is this false and deceptive spirit, so that still the learned of old would understand, nor all the impious understand yet. Because the devil himself has changed religion with such subtlety; and that he may deceive more easily, he mixes true and false under the name of Christianity, so that he may encourage his own preachers, better at supposing than believing.

‘For as in the beginnings of the Catholic Church after Christ ascended into the heavens, the Apostles’ of whom ‘this was said’ nearly till now, ‘it was the same with them as before He ascended; already the Church began to suffer; and the suffering increased after His departure; and many tribulations were imposed on the Church,’  so that publicly, she shed her own blood for Christ’s name, which Jews were forbidden to name Him.

‘We recognize it will be so, when the Antichrist comes. Let many in various places or regions hold back from the heretics and often’ from the nations; ‘but he says that “days”, “ten” of “tribulation”, “you will have”.’

What he says is “ten days”; he specifies all the time in this world’s ages, because we say “ten” as a way of speaking, like “hundred” and like “thousand” — that is, a perfect number means the whole of this world’s ages. As if he said “you will have tribulation”, as if only ten days; who have it to the end.’

‘If the present is toward a future of everlasting blessedness, you who tolerate them may ponder the evils; you will doubtless think of them so little, so quickly crossed, as if they were ten days. Therefore the Apostle also says, “For I reckon that the sufferings of this time are not worthy to be compared with the glory to come, that shall be revealed in us.” [Rom. 8:18]‘

‘And so He comforts His own, and says, “Be thou faithful until death, and I will give thee the crown of life.”‘

‘And in the Gospel, the Lord spoke, “But he that shall persevere to the end, he shall be saved.” [Matt. 24:13] For He can make it so that’ whoever lives ‘wickedly,’ (1) also at the end of life will return to true penitence, and believe rightly in God and recognize Mother Church; although the same hour that he does this he would die, he would be absolved from his sin. Again he can also make it so that whoever lives well, if he goes away from justice at the end of his life, he cannot be saved if he dies like that, because he did not persevere until the end. But whoever in the end will be justified or damned, the Lord judges the same way as He finds them in the end. He damns or crowns.

Nicholas of Lyra
 
Therefore it is added: Fear none That is, protected by me, you will cross to glory through martyrdom. Behold, the devil will cast To do this, he will incite the infidels. that you may be tried To be tested as gold in a furnace. This test, moreover, is not the intention of the Devil; he does not intend good for those he oppresses, but it is God’s intention to draw the good from evil things. By the cruelty of tyrants he lifts up the patience and the crown of the martyrs. and you shall have tribulation ten days This is explained in many ways: in one way by referring to the tribulations of the Church generally, signified here by ten days; in another manner the tribulation of ten days signifies a perfect number and the first distinction of numbers; the following numbers are replications of ten and of its parts, as it is said in Bk. 3 of The Physics. For eleven is one and ten; twelve is two and ten, and so forth to twenty, which is two-times ten. Thirty is three-times ten, and so forth. In another way, it refers to the ten laws of the Decalogue. That is, the tribulation of the Church will last as long as the observance of laws of the Decalogue, which is to the end of the world. It is also possible to be explained in another way, so that the ten days are understood as ten years, according to Ez. 4:6 “One day for each year.” Perchance the persecution of the church in Smyrna lasted that long. until death To endure by the faith itself. and I will give thee the crown of life That is, where death does not have a place.

All that is mentioned is recorded in the writing of The Martyrdom of St. Polycarp . The ten days might have ended when St. Polycarp was martyred on Febuary 22, 156AD.
