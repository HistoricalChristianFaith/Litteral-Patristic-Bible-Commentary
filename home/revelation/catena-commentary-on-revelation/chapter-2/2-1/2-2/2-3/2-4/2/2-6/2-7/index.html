He, that hath an ear, let him hear what the Spirit saith to the churches: To him, that overcometh, I will give to eat of the tree of life, which is in the paradise of my God.
 
 
 
 
St. Methodius
 
For they (the Jews) have not understood that the tree of life which Paradise once bore, now again the Church has produced for all, even the ripe and comely fruit of faith. Such fruit it is necessary that we bring when we come to the judgment-seat of Christ, on the first day of the feast; for if we are without it we shall not be able to feast with God, nor to have part, according to John, in the first resurrection. For the tree of life is wisdom first begotten of all. "She is a tree of life to them that lay hold upon her," says the prophet; "and happy is every one that retaineth her.(Prov. 3:18)" "A tree planted by the waterside, that will bring forth his fruit in due season;(Ps. 1:3)" that is, learning and charity and discretion are imparted in due time to those who come to the waters of redemption. He that hath not believed in Christ, nor hath understood that He is the first principle and the tree of life, since he cannot show to God his tabernacle adorned with the most goodly of fruits, how shall he celebrate the feast? How shall he rejoice? Desirest thou to know the goodly fruit of the tree? Consider the words of our Lord Jesus Christ, how pleasant they are beyond the children of men. Good fruit came by Moses, that is the Law, but not so goodly as the Gospel. For the Law is a kind of figure and shadow of things to come, but the Gospel is truth and the grace of life. Pleasant was the fruit of the prophets, but not so pleasant as the fruit of immortality which is plucked from the Gospel. [Banquet of the 10 Virgins 9.3]

Tyconius
 
“Who conquers, I will grant to eat from the tree of life,” that is, from the fruit of the cross, “which is in the paradise of my God.” The Church is to be regarded as paradise, for “all things were done in figure,” and Adam was, “ the shadow of the one to come,” as the Apostle teaches.(Rom. 5:14) Indeed, the tree of life is the wisdom of God, the Lord Jesus Christ, who hung on the cross. In the Church and in the spiritual paradise, He gives to the faithful food of life and the Sacrament of the celestial bread, of which you read, “Wisdom is the tree of life to those who embrace her.(Prov. 3:18)”

Oecumenius
 
He, that hath an ear, that is, who is obedient and submissive to the divine laws. let him hear what the Spirit saith to the churches: He said Spirit either because the events of the Revelation were being carried out in the Spirit, or he calls Christ Spirit, as in fact he is and is understood to be God, just as of course he is also called the son of man, as he is perceived as man. For the Godhead as a whole is called Spirit.

St. Andrew of Caesarea

Every person has a carnal ear, but only the spiritual person possesses a spiritual one, which was bestowed on Isaiah. He (Christ) promised to grant to such a victor in the war against the demons to eat from the tree of life, that is, to partake of the blessings of the future age, for periphrastically, eternal life is meant by the tree. Christ is each of these, as Solomon says and the present apostle writes elsewhere; the one (Solomon) concerning wisdom, saying, It is the tree of life, and likewise the other (the Apostle John) concerning Christ says, He is God and eternal life. Therefore, if we are allowed to attain them, let us achieve victory over the passions, for the rewards will certainly follow the pains by the grace and philanthropy of our Lord Jesus Christ with whom glory belongs, to the Father together with the Holy Spirit, unto the ages of ages. Amen.

Apringius of Beja
 
He has spoken of the toil of the Church: he has described the perversity of the heretics. Now to those still in their failures, he exhorts to repentance, and he promises rewards after this toil to those who are victorious, so that, entering into paradise, they might freely eat from the tree of life, for which Adam was expelled from paradise, lest he eat anything from it. And so he says, “which is in the paradise of my God,” where, namely, the wind breathes life, where the mysteries give virtue, and he furnishes the fruit of the tree of life, that is, and eternity which does not fade away.

St. Beatus of Liébana
 
As He clearly shows Himself by the secret of the mystery being told here, He says, “He that hath an ear, let him hear what the Spirit saith to the churches.” And the Lord in the Gospel says, “The words that I have spoken to you are spirit and life.” Therefore, he who is opened to the faith “hath an ear”, who gives the sharpest listening of the inner man through trustfulness of the good, is able to hear the words of the same divine speech which the Holy Spirit utters.

“To him that overcometh, I will give to eat of the tree of life, which is in the paradise of My God.”

The prize after labor of those who overcome is guaranteed; the labor of the Church is foretold, the perversity of the heretics described, the delinquents encouraged to penance; for the tree of life, over which Adam was expelled from Paradise, is not what he supposed; in that place, those who enter Paradise would receive what you freely accept taking. So He says, “which is in the paradise of My God”, where they breathe the air of life, where they win the secret virtue, and the apple of the tree of life supplies an unfading eternity of life. Therefore, it is well said, ““To him that overcometh, I will give to eat of the tree of life, which is in the paradise of My God.”

[Isidore:] ‘Paradise is the Church’, to which no one enters except he who has known Christ with a pure mind; has followed His footsteps with all his mind, all his heart, and all his strength; and has loved his neighbor as himself.

For Paradise is a figure of the Church. And the first Man, Adam, is a shadow of the future. And the second Man, Christ, is the Sun of Justice, who illuminates the shadow of our blindness. And as the Apostle says, “The First Adam is of the earth, earthly; the Second Adam is of heaven, heavenly.” [1 Cor. 15:47] Surely, in this way there are two Adams in the Church, the earthly and the heavenly; and as the earthly is, so are we earthly; and as the heavenly is, so are we heavenly.

Because Adam is divided into two parts: old and new. The Old [Adam] is he who is not given the tree of life to touch, because he will not put off the old man, that is, the carnal man. The New Adam is he who is mingled with the overcomers in Christ, has the power of the tree of life, and who always will have it, and though not yet out of his body, still his spirit is mingled in Christ. Because if those who overcome are promised the tree of life, many also have overcome before in Christ. Because not all are dead, but those who have sinned in the likeness of Adam’s transgression. The rest, who remain in the image and likeness of God and are remembered, are said to live. They live because God is not God of the dead but of the living. And so in Adam there are two parts, and out of Adam they are prefigured in news of the future: the one who will confess that he has sinned, and will live; the other who does not recover from the snares of the devils by whom he was captured, from whom the road to the tree of life is hidden.

Which parts at once [Adam] begins to beget; and of [his] two [sons] offering sacrifices to God, in fact one is leasing and the other displeasing. One is prostrate and singleminded, humbly offering the sacrifice, dying at his brother’s hand. The other is obtuse — that is, thoughtless — and envious in offering, since after killing his brother he appeared defiant. But the succession and progeny of Cain and Abel, and both of the two parts, is declared in Scripture.

For so the Lord says: “Now, therefore, cursed shalt thou be upon the earth, which hath opened her mouth and received the blood of thy brother at thy hand.” [Gen. 4:11] He says “earth”, “man”. All parricides are from Cain, or he has upheld the hatreds of brothers in their exercise and completion. In Cain and Abel, recognize all the whole people existing within the Church: one good, the other evil; one who does harm, the other who suffers it.

Here is a city which is founded under the name of [Cain's] son. So what kind of city is it that Cain constructed under his son’s name, if not of what you will know — founded to be the impious in this life? They have an earthly beginning and end, where nothing more is hoped for than what is seen.

Truly, the saints are strangers and pilgrims on the earth. From which also, Abel is a pilgrim on the earth, so to speak — that is, the holy people do not make a city, for their city is above, whatever [city] here they may seem to be a citizen of; to which they wander, until the time of His Kingdom may come. Truly, [Adam] speaks thus of Abel’s succession: “God hath given me another seed, for Abel whom Cain slew.” This he says of the succession, he says of the Church.

Look, therefore, because God did not forbid the tree of life to all of Adam, but to part [of him]. For Adam lives in eternity, which could not happen without a taste of that tree. But he tasted it, trusting error. What if it were not a figure of the future, but only Adam, for which the Lord separated those who approached the tree of life after the sense of death, those not tasting may have lived?

For if the Lord is not revered, one would not be able to live without his thought, even if he would have eaten the tree’s trunk all his life. But this is made into a figure, so that the truth lies open to us in the Church. For the Body and Blood of the Lord is life, as He says: “He that eateth my flesh, and drinketh my blood, hath everlasting life: and I will raise him up in the last day.” [John 6:55]

Surely not all who receive Communion have eternal life? No, because it is written, “For he that eateth and drinketh unworthily” the Body and Blood of the Lord “eateth and drinketh judgment to himself”. [1 Cor. 11:29] For the part that tests itself and understands in what way he may eat — he alone eats the tree of life.

Surely the part that is blinded and has not turned toward Christ’s light of life, though he may eat this bread, no doubt has the tree of life hidden [from him]. As God says to Job, “Have you hidden light from the wicked?” [Job 38:15 - Vetus Latina]

Therefore, God conceals the tree of life — that is, the True Cross — from those pursuing earthly gains, but certainly making a lukewarm life, as it is indeed written, “[Blind the heart of this people, and make their ears heavy, and shut their eyes,] lest they see with their eyes, and hear with their ears, and understand with their heart, and be converted and healed.” [Isaiah 6:10] Against them, Paradise is closed by God with a wall of fire, as He says in Zachariah about the Church: “And I will be to it, saith the Lord, a wall of fire round about; and I will be in glory in the midst thereof.” The tree of life is in the Church; it is shown openly in the description of the Church in this book. And the tree of life is Paradise and the Church and the worthy penance — that is, Christ’s Cross — which is seen to be carried by many — but they do not follow the Lord.

Out of either part of the river, it is said, the tree of life making twelve fruit for each of the months. These are the Lord of Paradise’s [fruit], and his tree grants them to the overcomers.

Paradise is the Church. The tree of life is Christ crucified; out of either part of the river, understand the two Testaments of the Law and the Gospel, or the wave of Baptism. The twelve months are the Twelve Apostles. These He grants the overcomers, and His Cross to His enemies; of whom God is the womb, and Christ to their beds; the other to the ignorant who, because they do not adore our Crucified, but the head of the Beast who was as if killed — that is, as if it were Christ crucified — for which they have only the name in common with us, and deny Him in their deeds. This tree of life hides from all of those.

THE FIRST CHURCH ENDS.

St. John of Damascus
 
“God planted the tree of life and the tree of knowledge. The tree of knowledge was for trial, and proof, and exercise of man’s obedience and disobedience. It was named thus or else it was because to those who partook of it was given power to know their own nature. Now this is a good thing for those who are mature, but an evil thing for the immature and those whose appetites are too strong… The tree of life, on the other hand, was a tree having the energy that is the cause of life, or to be eaten only by those who deserve to live and are not subject to death… The tree of life may be understood as that more divine thought that has its origin in the world of sense, and the ascent through that to the originating and constructive cause of all. And this was the name He gave to every tree, implying fullness and indivisibility, and conveying only participation in what is good. But by the tree of the knowledge of good and evil, we are to understand that sensible and pleasurable food which, sweet though it seems, in reality brings him who partakes of it into communion with evil.” [Orthodox Faith 2.11]

Benedict Pereira

In these words is signified that it is not all who have spiritual ears, such as they ought to have, to hear the Word of God. As says Isaiah (42:20) of certain people, 'Who is blind, and who is deaf but my people who have eyes open and see not, and ears open and hear not.' Matthew also says that our Lord exclaimed, 'He who hath ears to hear let him hear.' Moreover interior and spiritual hearing consists of three things. First, the application and attention of the mind to the Word of God: that which is preached is to be heard and understood. Next an inclination of the understanding to believe it. Then a pious affection and propensity of the will to love and embrace it," &c.
