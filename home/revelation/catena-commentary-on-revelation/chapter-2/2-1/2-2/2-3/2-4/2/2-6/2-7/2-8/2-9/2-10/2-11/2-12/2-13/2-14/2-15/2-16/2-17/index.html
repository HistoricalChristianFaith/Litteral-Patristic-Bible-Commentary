He, that hath an ear, let him hear what the Spirit saith to the churches: To him that overcometh, I will give the hidden manna, and will give him a white counter, and in the counter, a new name written, which no man knoweth, but he that receiveth it.
 
 
 
St. Irenaeus of Lyons
 
But as the engrafted wild olive does not certainly lose the substance of its wood, but changes the quality of its fruit, and receives another name, being now not a wild olive, but a fruit-bearing olive, and is called so; so also, when man is grafted in by faith and receives the Spirit of God, he certainly does not lose the substance of flesh, but changes the quality of the fruit [brought forth, i.e.] of his works, and receives another name, showing that he has become changed for the better, being now not [mere] flesh and blood, but a spiritual man, and is called such. [Against Heresies 5.10.2]

St. Victorinus
 
To him that overcomes I will give the hidden manna, and I will give him a white stone. The hidden manna is immortality; the white gem is adoption to be the son of God; the new name written on the stone is Christian.

The fourth class intimates the nobility of the faithful, who labour daily, and do greater works. But even among them also He shows that there are men of an easy disposition to grant unlawful peace, and to listen to new forms of prophesying; and He reproves and warns the others to whom this is not pleasing, who know the wickedness opposed to them: for which evils He purposes to bring upon the head of the faithful both sorrows and dangers.

Tyconius
 
This manna is the invisible Bread which came down from Heaven, which indeed was made man, so that ‘man might eat the bread of angels.(Ps. 78:25)’ And the figure of this was indicated beforehand in the manna given in the desert. Whoever ate of that bread were said by the Lord to have died, because remaining faithless, they did not eat of this hidden and uniquely spiritual manna by which Christ offers immortality to the faithful. For he said, “Unless you eat the flesh of the Son of man and drink His blood you will not have eternal life in you.(John 6:53)” For, indeed, whoever at that time was able to eat spiritually, they were worthy to obtain the same immortality, as were Moses and others. As the apostle reaches, “They are the same spiritual food.(1Cor. 10:3)” For the visible manna was in no way detrimental to those who used the bodily food in a spiritual manner. So also now, the spiritual manna of the Lord’s body does not profit persons in the present if they receive it unworthily, for “they eat and drink judgment upon themselves.(1Cor. 11:29)”

St. Jerome
 
You shall be called by a new name (Isaiah 62.2). A new name is deserving of a new son. So in the Apocalypse, To him that overcometh will I give to eat of the hidden manna, and will give him a white stone, and in the stone a new name written (2.17). The new name is that of Christians." [Homily 25 On the Psalms]

St. Caesarius of Arles
 
“And I will give to him a white stone.” That is the body made white by baptism. “And on the stone is written a new name,” that is the knowledge of the Son of man. “which no one knows except him who receives it.” That is, no on knows it except through revelation, and for that reason it is said of the Jews, “For had they known, they would not have crucified the Lord of glory. [Commentary on Revelation]
 
Therefore, God always rains manna from Heaven for us; and from God descend those words which are spoken to us. Thus, when we receive such manna, it is always given to us from Heaven. Now an examination of the word tells us the same thing. Manna is interpreted as ‘What is this?(Exodus 16:15)’ See whether the very power of the name does not provoke you to learn it, so that when you hear the law of God read in church you may always ask and say to the teachers, ‘What is this? This is what the manna indicates. [Sermon 102.3]

St. Andrew of Caesarea
 
The Bread of Life is the hidden manna, the One who descended from heaven for us and has become edible. Figuratively, the future good things are also called manna, inasmuch as they are coming down from above, just as the heavenly Jerusalem. He says those who are victorious against the devil will obtain these things. And they will receive both the small white stone, that is, the victorious being deemed worthy of the right portion, and the new name which is unknown in the present life. For eye has not seen and ear has not heard and mind has not grasped the good things of the future and the new name which the saints will inherit.

St. Beatus of Liébana 
 
‘That is about the Bread Who came down from Heaven.’  Of which Bread, in daily prayer we say, “Give us this day our daily bread.” [Lk. 11:3] We say “our”; but it is with Him, and unless we will ask for it, we do not receive it. ‘The figure’ of His Bread ‘was the manna in the wilderness,’ but it is not known to all. For many of its eaters died, as the Lord says: “They ate manna in the wilderness, and they died.” [John 6:49] Out of them, others ate manna and did not die, as Moses and the rest. For He disapproved this bread, but He showed the hidden one.

On the other hand, it was this same Bread Who now is in the Church. As it is written, “And all of them ate the same spiritual food,” [1 Cor. 10:3] and yet in the same way he has eaten spiritual bread. But it is not the Bread of Life to all; “Therefore, whosoever shall eat this bread… unworthily… eats… judgment on himself.” [1 Cor. 11:27-28]

That is, whoever reads the Scriptures eats the Bread; but if he does not do what he reads, he eats judgment on himself. We read the Scriptures for this — so that we may come to know Christ; and through Christ, the Trinity that is One God, we may believe rightly.

This is solid food; this is the hidden manna, as it is written: “He had given them the bread of heaven; a man ate the bread of angels.” [Ps. 77:24] Because it is believed to be He Who “in the beginning” was with the Father and the Holy Spirit, always equal; and the angels may enjoy the sight of His Divinity; now “the Word was made flesh, and dwelt among us… This is the bread which came down from Heaven. If anyone shall eat of it, he will not die, but have eternal life.” [John 1:14, 6:50]

This is the hidden bread, Who is not given except to legitimate contestants, and those who persevere in the love of God and neighbor. Here is the manna which nobody receives except he who asks for it.

That nobody asks for, except if God in free pity should have given him light (illuminaverit), and should have drawn him to penitence. Because according to the Apostle, “Therefore he hath mercy on whom he will; and whom he will, he hardeneth.” [Rom. 9:18] ‘He has mercy from His great goodness; he hardens from no unfairness,’  because there is no unfairness in God. But each one is bound by a snare of his own sins.

Like mud and wax, for example.  In the equal heat that the Sun has, mud tightens [as it dries, hardens, and contracts], and wax softens. Whose gets the blame for it? The Sun or the mud? Far be it from the Sun, which does not change its equality of brightness. So neither is God to blame.

Of course the Sun’s essence is in radiance, and each one’s work is in its manifestation, as it is written: “So let your light shine before men, that they may see your good works, and glorify your Father who is in Heaven.” [Mt. 5:16]

But it is not empty to examine that figure of speech about wax, the nature of which is known to proceed from virginity. [Virgin worker bees.] But mud is sin, surely, as it is written: “The dog is returned to his vomit; and the sow that was washed, to the sow wallowing in the mud.” [2 Peter 2:22] The “pigs” are those who do not yet believe the Gospel, and keep heading back into the mud of unbelief and vices. Therefore, God is not to blame, “Who will have all men to be saved, and to come to the knowledge of the Truth.” [1 Tim. 2:4] Therefore, the manna is given to those who believe the Gospel, and to those who are eager in their minds to do what is written in it.

Those who came out of Egypt, they all ate this bread; but not all entered the Promised Land. And now, all those who come out from the Egypt of this age (which is called “tribulation” in Latin), eat this bread, but not all enter Paradise. For some die every day from famine, ignorant of the way through this spiritual world, as if through the wilderness. For if they had gotten to the way — that is, to Christ, who says “I Am the way” [John 14:6]; and if they had eaten this bread, by no means would they have died in the wilderness — that is, in ignorance of books; but by Jesus for leader, they would have walked, rejoicing, on a flat trail into the land of Paradise.

Because as ‘the Apostle says, “For in Christ Jesus neither circumcision availeth any thing, nor uncircumcision, but a new creature.” [Gal. 6:15] For there are no great merits if what is outside and facing toward us is urged into the body; but it [there is merit in] considering what is urged into the mind. For to despise the present world and not to love the transitory; to arrange the mind into inward humility toward God and neighbor; to serve with patience against pushy indignities, and repel the pain of malice from your heart by keeping patience; to give of your own to the needy and not desire another’s; to love friends in God and because of God to love those who are enemies; to mourn with neighbors in affliction and not to exult in the death of an enemy. This is the new creature, as the same Teacher of the Gentiles asks disciples for a watchful eye, among other things, saying, “If then any be in Christ a new creature, the old things are passed away; behold, all things are made new.” [2 Cor. 5:17]‘

Therefore, the hidden manna was given to them without doubt; to them, ‘the tree of life’  that is in the Paradise of God — that is, it is ordered to touch the cross of Christ in the Church. To them, it is said, “He that believeth in me, as the scripture saith, Out of his belly shall flow rivers of living water.” [John 7:38] Those members are the Apostles, who with Jesus as head — that is, Jesus as leader — walk to the promised heavenly kingdom.

‘Of course, it concerns the old man to seek the present world; to love the transitory out of concupiscence; to build up the mind into pride; not to have patience; to think of neighbors’ injury out of the pain of malice; not to give the needy his own; to seek increase from others; to love nobody purely’ because of God; ‘to return enemies their enmity; to rejoice in neighbors’ affliction. These are the old established man, who is seen to be’ drawn ‘to corruption from the root;’  and these are not yet given the hidden manna, because they have not found Christ the Way. Truly, His Church says, “I will manifest myself to him.” [Jn. 14:21]

“And I will give him a white counter” — ‘that is,’ (1) ‘a body dressed in white from Baptism.’  For the counter of stone is the white one of which ‘the Apostle says, “Be you also as living stones built up into the temple of God.” [1 Peter 2:5]‘

‘And precious stones too;’ they are understood ‘as the confessors, apostles, priests [sacerdotes] and all the just.’ Through Moses, these stones were ordered to be offered up into a temple of ‘God [Exod. 25:3?], so that nobody may despair of salvation.’ ‘Another’ offered ‘”gold”,’ in the spiritual ‘sense’ — that is, mystical understanding in the Church; ‘another “silver” speech’, that is, allegorical or moral understanding; another a “brass” voice, that is, historic understanding.

For Holy Scripture ‘is understood in three ways: first as historically, second as allegorically [tropologice], third as mystically. And “historically” is joined to the letter, “allegorically” is joined to moral knowledge, “mystically” is joined to spiritual intelligence. Therefore, it is right’ thus ‘to hold faith’ in the Catholic Church, ‘so that we must’ read scriptures historically, ‘interpret them morally, and understand them spiritually.’  Therefore, it is well said, “I will give him a white counter” — that is, ‘I will make him sit with the powers of My people, who are the Apostles, and I will [give him a throne of glory for his inheritance]‘.

“And My Name is written upon the counter” — ‘that is, the Mystery of the Son of Man.’  As if He would say, “I will manifest Myself to him.” [John 14:21]

“Which no man knoweth but he that receiveth it.” ‘For let the hypocrites be seen to have’ what it is not given to them to understand, as it is written: “Because to you it is given to know the mysteries of the kingdom of Heaven;” [Mt. 13:11] — ‘and to learn My secret, to those whom I see labor in My love.’ To which secret, the Prophet referred when he would say, “The hidden things of Thy wisdom Thou hast made manifest to me.” [Ps. 50:8/51:6] ‘Surely it is not given them who preach their own words, not Mine; and they persecute Me when they hold you in despite.’

And yet, of which also it is said to Ezekiel, ‘“Son of man, go to the house of Israel…and thou shalt speak My words to them.” [Ezek. 2:3,7] What the Lord says to him, “thou shalt speak My words to them” — what else than a bridle does He impose on his mouth? Nor what earlier would he not hear at home that he would presume to say abroad?’

‘Yet the false prophets’ that are the hypocrites and heretics, speak their own words ‘and not the Lord’s,’ as also in their time ‘they spoke’ their own ‘words and not the Lord’s, of which it is written, “Hearken not to the words of the prophets that prophesy to you, and deceive you. They speak a vision of their own heart, and not out of the mouth of the Lord.” [Jeremiah 23:16]‘

And the reverse: “I have not spoken to them, yet they prophesied.” [Jeremiah 23:21]

‘Which from the event is likewise gathered, because also whoever the Expositor in explanation of sacred utterances, as perhaps to please listeners, composes anything deceptive and says his own words and not the Lord’s — even if he would lie to his own study, pleasing or seducing himself.

Yet if he would think they have discovered in the Lord’s words requiring virtue something else than that through them, yet still if it would require by his understanding the building up of charity toward others, they are the Lord’s words that he tells.’

Because it is written, “Knowledge puffs up, but charity builds up.” [1 Cor. 8:1]

‘Hence John says, “He who saith that he knoweth him, and keepeth not his commandments, is a liar, and the Truth is not in him.” [1 John 2:4]‘

And again, “He that saith he is in the light, and hateth his brother, is in darkness even until now.” [1 John 2:9]

‘For if’ the Mystery of God were to have been ‘known’ by a hypocrite, he ‘never’ would have cut down God in his household.

["I speak in a mystery... for if they had known it, they would never have crucified the Lord of glory." [1 Cor. 2:7-8]]

HERE ENDS THE THIRD CHURCH.

Bulus al-Bushi
 
Hidden manna does not mean the physical manna, which was eaten by the corporeal children of Israel. They died because there was no ultimate purpose connected with it (Jn 6:58). The white garment did not mean a physical garment but the radiance of the souls and the bodies of the righteous at the general resurrection, just as He testified when He said, “the righteous will shine forth like the sun in the kingdom of their father.(Mt 13:43)”

Nicholas of Lyra
 
To him that overcometh, I will give the hidden manna Manna figuratively; clearly it was given corporeally to the children of Israel in the desert(Ex. 16), but the true manna is He Himself, who says in Jn. 6, “Truly truly I say to you, it was Moses who gave you the bread from Heaven, but it is my Father who gives you the true bread from Heaven.” And it is added, “I am the bread of Life.” This is the Heavenly manna that refreshes the saints inside and outside, as was said previously, and it is called “hidden” to persons of this present life, according to which is said in Ps. 31:19, “Oh how abundant is your goodness that you have laid up for those who fear you.”

Francisco Ribera

new name: Things derive their name from that which they are; and every one has his name from the good he possesses. When therefore we shall be the sons of God, coming to the promised inheritance after a new manner, then we shall have a new name from that which we then begin to be. For that is called new which is made anew, and which begins in a new manner; John 13:34, 'A new commandment give I unto you; that ye love one another as I have loved you.' There was indeed an old commandment of love; but the manner, 'as I have loved you was new, as Cyril teaches, and indeed the common interpretation. So also 1 John, chap, 2, calls the same commandment old and new. So also in Isaiah 65: 17, 'Behold I create new heavens and a new earth, and the former shall not be remembered.' These remarks are all confirmed by Isaiah 62:2, where it is said to the church, And you shall be called by a new name, which the mouth of the Lord shall name.' This new name is the church, as is observed by Jerome and others; a name which nevertheless is often found in the Old Testament but after the advent of Christ it began to be held in a new manner since at that time a faithful people possessed it which before possessed it not.

Rupert of Deutz

'He that hath ears to hear,' &c. In this passage to hear what the Spirit says, is, not to cease from sound doctrine in order to destroy the doctrine of Balaam, and to exercise a holy knowledge, and always to remember the sentence of the Lord pronounced in the Gospel, 'Woe unto you, lawyers, because ye have taken away the key of knowledge! Ye have neither entered in yourselves, and those which were entering in ye have forbidden.' For those who, under the name of Christianity, either cherish sins or suffer them to be cherished, thinking that a divine name alone will suffer to salvation, (as did they who, trusting in the words of a He, proclaimed 'the temple of the Lord, the temple of the Lord, the temple of the Lord are we;' and who yet had made that temple a den of thieves,) have never entered into a true knowledge. For if they had entered in; if they had carefully attended to the words of Holy Scripture, or had been willing to do so, they would have known that the profession of a divine name is of no avail; but that, as in the case of Balaam, it may be supplanted by wickedly teaching others to eat things sacrificed unto idols, and to commit fornication.

Haydock Bible
 
To him that overcometh, I will give the hidden manna; a happiness in heaven, which the eye hath not seen, &c. --- And a white[1] stone, with a new name written, as a mark of the happiness promised to all those who shall conquer. An allusion to the custom of giving a white stone to those that were tried and acquitted, and also to persons promoted to a dignity; and a black stone to such as were found guilty. See Acts xxvi. 10. (Witham) --- This new name is the eternal recompense, unknown and despised by worldlings, but esteemed by the faithful, who know the excellence of the rewards promised by God. (Calmet)
